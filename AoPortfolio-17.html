Description:

The Powerpuff Girls is an television series that centers around 3 super powered girls and their scientist

father/creator. Throughout the series, the girls fight crime in the city of Townsville while their father

tries to give them a normal life. However, due to the nature of their superpowers, they have become

celebrities in the city, which makes living a normal life somewhat challenging.

Due to their celebrity status, their family dynamics have to keep alternating between a normal family

structure and a celebrity family structure. I have chosen to focus on this aspect by exploring ways to

separate open and closed spaces as well as ways to control facade permeability by light and view.

Scenario:

The girls recently had an accident from their crime fighting hobby. Although it wasn’t serious, the professor

overreacted like the usual and decided to move away to get away from the stress. The professor

just doesn’t want the girls to get hurt, so he’s trying to protect them the best he can. Due to this,

he has to work from home to still be able to support for the family.

Design:

The main ideas to take away from this scenario is the fact that the professor needs to work at home,

while at the same time protecting his daughters. The design of this unit typology allows him to accomplish

that by designing his lab around the main body of the house, essentially creating a protective

barrier in which he can work from.

Description:

Dexter is an television series that centers around Dexter Morgan, a blood spatter pattern analyst who

also leads a secret life as a serial killer. Dexter tries to live 2 separate lives while keeping his darker

life a secret. Despite his hobby of killing people, he has a normal job and a loving family, but neither

of his other lives will understand his hobby. Throughout the series, his normal life starts to suspect his

murder life, but never quite finds enough evidence to reveal his dark life. I have decided to use this

plot formula as a characteristic in the programming of the housing by providing various moments in

which the architecture hints at a space to another space, but never quite reveals how to get there.

Scenario:

Dexter is becoming close enough to Rita that he finally moves in with her. However, after doing so

they find that their current house is too small. Dexter has always been very busy with his work and

hobby, however now he has to add being a family man without involving the two together on top of

that. Due to this he finds that having a place at home in which he can work alone can help cut down

on his commute time, thus allowing him to juggle around his 3 lives.

Design:

The main ideas to take away from this scenario is the fact that Dexter needs to work in his house

while being separated from his family. That being said, he still wants to be a part of his family and

maybe subconsciously wants his family to find out about his secret hobby. The design of this unit

typology allows that by having all the private rooms extrude into a central living area from 2 side walls

that define the central space. This hides the function of the rooms in plain view, allowing Dexter to

work freely in them while still being involved within the house.

Description:

Hamlet is a tragedy written by William Shakespeare that dramatizes the revenge that prince

Hamlet seeks on king Claudius for murdering his father. In the story, Hamlet’s uncle Claudius

murders Hamlet’s father and marries his mother Gertrude to steal the throne and control the

kingdom, in which Hamlet plans to exact his revenge by trying to kill Claudius. Throughout the

play, a continuous game of cat and mouse unfolds as hamlet tries to reveal Claudius’s evil deed

and Claudius trying to stay innocent. The characteristic of this conflict can be translated into

programming in terms of territory, thus i have chosen to explore different ways to divide territory

and different uses of partitions.

Scenario:

After a dispute between Hamlet and his uncle, Lady Gertrude decides a family vacation to their

other home outside of the kingdom would release some tension. Hamlet and Claudius are still

mad at each other and are not on speaking terms, but since they have to live under they same

roof they decide to mark their own space to keep the other one out so they can plot against

each other. Gertrude thinks this is childish of them, but this eventually turns into a territorial war

to control the vacation home.

Design:

The main ideas to take away from this scenario is that both Hamlet and Claudius wants to be

alone while Gertrude wants them to be together. This is done by dividing each floor of the house

into three areas with two walls. One area is hamlet, one area is claudious, and the central area

is a communal space that Gertrude resides in as she just want them to be a family. As the house

changes levels, the areas changes sizes, symbolizing the internal territorial conflict within the

house.
