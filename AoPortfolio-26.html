As a continuation of my thesis, I delve deeper into exploring the capabilities of Unreal Engine 4. This time, instead of simulating people, I decided to explore the photonic simulation that is real time photorealistic lighting.

This is an especially useful concept within architectural design as it allows the designer to create many iterations of renderings in a reletively short amount of time. This allows me to better utilize my time as well as allows me to fully utilize photorealistic renderings during the design phase instead of only after.

Within time, the goal is to apply this technique with the crowd simulation workflow from my thesis, thus allowing the rapid production of fully populated and photorealistic architectural spaces that can be utilized within every phase of architectural design.
