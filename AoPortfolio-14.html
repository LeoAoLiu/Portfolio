Every summer, the second-year undergraduate class at Waterloo Architecture produces, writes, and performs a play for their Cultural History course. This year, We were challenged to re-imagine and combine Mary Shelley’s original 1818 version of Frankenstein with Captain John Franklin’s infamous 1848 voyage to the Canadian Arctic. My main roll in the production of Erebus & Terror was the Design, Coordination, and Production of 7 Lanterns used by the Sailors throughout the play, which acted as a symbol for their lives as well as playing a large part in the lighting of the play.
