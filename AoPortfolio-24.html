Every so often, I try to learn a new skill by working on a personal project in my free time. In past Coops, I self taught myself Revit, photography, 3DS max, and vray, so for this coop I decided to learn videography. Coming from a photography background, videography seems like another level in terms of complexityâ€”Beyond the focus on the composition and lighting, one must also consider the temporality aspect of a moving picture.

However, time-lapses seems to be a natural stepping-stone between videos and photographs. It is essentially taking what I knew about photography, and applying it to many photos over time to form a video that not only captures the space, but also how it changes over time. Much like how digital rendering is the production of data from an virtual environment, photography is the capture of data from a physical environment. Time-lapses then takes this a step further to capture the temporality of this data as it changes throughout the day.

This project is a composition of 15,000+ pictures over a 5 month period. Each location in the video took 2-4 hours to shoot, and another 1-4 hours to post process. Following, is a series of sequences created from the video.
