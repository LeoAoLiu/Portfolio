To create this crowd simulation tool, it is important to first look at how to translate analog human behaviors into digital logic. This was achieved by situating human crowds in the context of complex systems, and investigating the concept of emergence, which is the tendency for systems to exhibit behaviors in greater complexity than the sum of its parts.

What is compelling about this phenomenon is how we can utilize relatively simple logic to generate complex behaviors. By investigating the idea of autonomous agents, we can simplify human behaviors into 3 main systems: The Sensory System, Decision Logic, and Pathfinding.

The sensory system allows the agent to see the environment, the decision logic allows them to choose an action, and the pathfinding allows them to perform said action.

This essentially breaks down the system of human crowds into the fundamentalsâ€”simple enough to be translated and approximated by machine logic. Then by reintroducing these agents within the virtual space, it becomes possible to rebuild the system from the bottom up.

This in turn allows the creation of different states for the agents depending on their choices, allowing the agents to function in a variety of spatial typologies.
